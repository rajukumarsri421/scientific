What is Carbon Sequestration?

Carbon sequestration is the process of capturing and storing atmospheric CO2 to mitigate climate change. This process occurs naturally through plants and oceans, and can also be engineered through technologies like carbon capture and storage (CCS).

Why is Carbon Sequestration Important?

The excessive accumulation of CO2 in the atmosphere is a major contributor to global warming. Carbon sequestration helps in reducing the greenhouse gas levels, thus slowing down the rate of climate change.

Methodology

This study explores various carbon sequestration techniques, including direct air capture (DAC) and geological storage. These methods focus on capturing CO2 emissions from industrial processes and storing them underground or in other stable forms.

Results - CO2 Reduction

Our study showed that through effective carbon capture methods, up to 40% of industrial CO2 emissions could be sequestered, significantly lowering the overall carbon footprint.

Results - Enhanced Concrete Strength

Carbon sequestration not only reduces CO2 but also improves the strength of concrete by carbonating calcium compounds. This makes it a dual-benefit technology for construction.

Challenges

While promising, carbon sequestration faces challenges such as high costs, energy consumption, and long-term storage risks. Overcoming these barriers is critical for widespread adoption.

Conclusion

Carbon sequestration technologies represent a viable solution to reduce CO2 emissions. With continuous improvements in efficiency and cost-effectiveness, they will be crucial in mitigating climate change.

Future Directions

Ongoing research into direct air capture, ocean fertilization, and afforestation could further enhance the efficiency of carbon sequestration. These innovations will help scale up efforts globally.
